Why should companies migrate to the cloud?

In recent years, more and more companies are choosing cloud server services such as Amazon, or Azure over the classic physical servers that companies had. According to a survey carried out by the IDC consulting firm, 43% of Spanish companies plan to migrate to the public cloud in the next two years. According to the study, the main reason for this migration is cost savings (50% of the companies surveyed cite it as the main benefit of the public cloud), followed by greater agility and flexibility to adapt to changes (33 %). Despite this, there are still some skeptical companies about migrating to cloud services. Companies that refuse to migrate to the public cloud argue that there are risks associated with this decision, such as the loss of control over data, security and privacy. However, these risks can be minimized by implementing proper security measures. In my opinion, migration to the public cloud is a wise decision for companies for the following reasons.

First, companies have reported a reduction in infrastructure costs in a average 25% after migrating to the cloud. This is because companies do not have to invest in hardware, software or personnel to manage servers, resources that can be used for other purposes. In addition, companies do not have to worry about the capacity of the servers, since it increases automatically according to the demand that the application or service that the company offers or consumes needs.

Second, migrating to the public cloud allows companies to be more agile and flexible in responding to change. This is because applications and services can be provisioned in minutes, rather than days or weeks. In addition, the maintenance or updating of software or hardware is no longer the responsibility of the companies, but is assumed by the cloud service provider.

However, the biggest doubt about the cloud is the issue of security, but this is not a reason not to migrate to the cloud. In fact, security is one of the main concerns for companies when migrating to the cloud. To minimize these risks, it is important for companies to select a reliable cloud service provider with a good reputation for security. In addition, it is important that companies implement adequate security measures, such as data encryption, the use of strong passwords, the implementation of firewalls and constant monitoring of servers.

In short, migrating to the public cloud is a smart decision for companies, as it allows cost savings, greater agility and flexibility, and minimizes security risks, in addition to other more organizational benefits such as the investment of the money saved in a better Product development. Taking into account these advantages but also the disadvantages, I would recommend companies to have their services in the cloud from the first instance.
